I like ground_truth much better than annotated. It's the terminology you'll hear frequently in ML and AI evaluation work, and it makes the role of that dataset explicit: these are the human-created answers that every model will be measured against.

That change also reinforces the story of the project:

Collect raw engineering incidents.
Create the ground truth through careful human annotation.
Validate the quality and consistency of those annotations.
Split the ground truth into training, validation, and held-out evaluation sets.
Evaluate prompt-only, RAG, LoRA, and other models against the same trusted ground truth.

I think that's a much stronger narrative for both the repository and interviews because it mirrors how production AI teams build reliable evaluation datasets.
