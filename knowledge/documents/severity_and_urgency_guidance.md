# Severity and Urgency Guidance

## Low

Use low severity for minor, stable conditions that do not indicate immediate equipment damage or operational danger.

Typical urgency: routine.

## Medium

Use medium severity when diagnosis or corrective action is needed soon but there is no direct evidence of immediate danger or severe equipment damage.

Typical urgency: within 24 hours.

## High

Use high severity when continued operation could damage equipment, worsen a leak, interrupt production, or create a meaningful operational hazard.

Typical urgency: immediate.

## Critical

Reserve critical severity for imminent danger, hazardous release, major safety risk, or severe equipment damage requiring emergency action.

Urgency must be immediate.

Do not classify an incident as low merely because the system has not yet shut down.
